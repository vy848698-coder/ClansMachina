Costs & Savings

Residential systems run roughly ₹55,000–70,000 per kW before subsidy (a 3 kW system is about ₹1.7–2.1 lakh). Larger plants cost less per kW.

A well-sized on-grid system with net metering can cut your bill by up to 90%. Most homes save lakhs over the 25-year life.

Typically 4–5 years for homes after subsidy, and 3.5–5 years for commercial plants. After that, the power is effectively free.

Yes. Panels are warranted for 25 years, so once the system pays for itself you enjoy two decades of near-free electricity.

PM Surya Ghar Subsidy

The central scheme that subsidises residential rooftop solar to promote clean energy and free up to 300 units/month for households.

Up to ₹60,000 for 2 kW, ₹78,000 for 3 kW, capped at ₹78,000 for larger residential systems.

Indian homeowners with a valid connection in the household's name, a shadow-free roof, and a registered-vendor install via the national portal.

Credited directly to your bank account, usually within a few weeks of commissioning, net-meter installation and inspection.
