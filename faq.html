Costs & Savings

What Solar Costs and Saves

As a guide, residential systems run roughly ₹55,000-70,000 per kW before subsidy (so a 3 kW system is about ₹1.7-2.1 lakh). Larger commercial plants cost less per kW. Your final price depends on roof type, brand and structure.

A well-sized on-grid system with net metering can cut your electricity bill by up to 90%. Most homes save lakhs over the 25-year life of the system.

Solar starts making strong sense from around ₹2,500/month. The higher your bill and daytime usage, the faster the payback.

Typically 4-5 years for homes (after subsidy) and 3.5-5 years for commercial plants. After that, the power is effectively free for 20+ years.

Yes. Panels are warranted to perform for 25 years, so once the system pays for itself you enjoy two decades of near-free electricity.

Grid tariffs climb 3-5% a year. Because your solar generation cost is fixed, your savings actually grow larger every year.

Extra capacity self-funded

It is the central government scheme that subsidises residential rooftop solar to promote clean energy and free up to 300 units/month of electricity for households.

Indian homeowners with a valid electricity connection in the household's name, a suitable shadow-free roof, and an installation done by a registered vendor through the national portal.

It is credited directly to your bank account, usually within a few weeks of commissioning, net-meter installation and inspection.

This particular subsidy is for residential rooftops. Commercial users instead benefit from accelerated depreciation (up to 40%) and lower per-unit costs; societies can use group net metering and RESCO models.

Typically your latest electricity bill, identity/address proof, and bank details. We prepare and file the application for you end to end.

It significantly reduces it — up to ₹78,000 for a 3 kW system. The balance is your investment, which the energy savings then repay in a few years.
