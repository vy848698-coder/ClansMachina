Funding Models

Fund It, or Pay As You Save

Pick the model that suits your society's finances — own the plant outright or go zero-investment.

CAPEX

Society-Funded

Funded from the society's sinking fund.

  • Society owns the plant and all savings
  • Lowest lifetime cost per unit
  • Payback typically 4-4.5 years
  • Maximum long-term benefit to members

Best for: societies with reserve funds

OPEX / RESCO

Zero Investment

We build & maintain; society buys the power.

  • ₹0 upfront — no capital outlay
  • Pay a fixed, discounted per-unit tariff
  • Savings from month one, zero O&M
  • Easy AGM approval, low risk

Best for: quick, hassle-free approval

HYBRID

Part-Funded

Blend reserves with financing.

  • Split capital between fund & EMI
  • Balances upfront outlay with ownership
  • Flexible to the society's cash position
  • Retains strong long-term savings

Best for: balancing cash & ownership

How It Works

Powering the Common Meter

Rooftop solar feeds your common-area loads first; surplus is handled through metering arrangements.

01

Common-Meter Solar

Generation is wired to the society's common-area meter, directly offsetting the lifts, pumps and lighting that run all day.

  • Daytime loads run on free solar
  • Immediate cut to the common-area bill
  • Simple metering, fast approvals
02

Group Net Metering

Surplus units are exported and credited across the society's connected meters — nothing generated goes to waste.

  • Credits shared across society meters
  • Maximises the value of every unit
  • Ideal for large terrace capacity
Site Suitability

Is Your Terrace Solar-Ready?

We assess every society against four checks before proposing a design.

01

Terrace Area

Shared terrace and tower-top space — about 100 sq.ft. per kW — sets the achievable system size.

02

Shadow Mapping

We map water tanks, lift rooms and parapets to place panels in shadow-free zones for peak yield.

03

Structural Load

The lightweight ~15 kg/sq.m. mounted system is well within a standard RCC terrace's capacity.

04

Meter Mapping

We map common-area meters to size the plant to actual lift, pump and lighting consumption.
