How It Works

Two Ways Your Rooftop Generates Value

Panels convert sunlight to DC; the inverter converts it to AC your building uses instantly. Surplus is handled one of two ways.

01

Net Metering

A bi-directional meter exports surplus daytime generation to the DISCOM and credits it against units you draw at night — so the grid acts as your battery.

  • Peak generation 11 AM-2 PM exported for credits
  • Evening consumption offset by banked units
  • Ideal when generation can exceed daytime load
02

Captive Consumption

Behind-the-meter solar feeds your load directly. With high daytime demand, every generated unit is consumed on-site — no export, maximum self-use.

  • 1 kW generates ~4 units/day (~120 units/month)
  • Best when load always exceeds generation
  • No bi-directional meter required
Site Suitability

Is Your Roof Solar-Ready?

Our engineers validate every site against four checks before design sign-off.

01

Structural Strength

A rooftop plant adds only ~15 kg/sq.m. We verify load capacity for RCC terraces and metal sheet roofs alike.

02

Roof Type & Mounting

RCC roofs use elevated hot-dip galvanised structures; metal sheet roofs use aluminium rail mounts — leak-proof and durable.

03

Shadow & Orientation

South-facing, shadow-free between 9 AM-4 PM, set at ~10° tilt in South India and 20°+ in the North for peak yield.

04

Space & Access

Roughly 100 sq.ft. per kW, with safe rooftop access for installation and scheduled preventive maintenance.
