How It Works

Two Ways to Set Up Your Home

Panels generate DC, the inverter makes it home-ready AC. Choose how you handle surplus and outages.

01

On-Grid + Net Metering

The most popular and lowest-cost setup. Surplus daytime power is exported to the grid for credits that offset your night-time usage.

  • Lowest cost, highest savings
  • Grid acts as your virtual battery
  • Eligible for PM Surya Ghar subsidy
02

Hybrid + Battery Backup

Adds a battery so essential loads keep running during power cuts — ideal for areas with unreliable supply.

  • Power backup during outages
  • Store daytime solar for evening use
  • Best for frequent power-cut areas
Roof Check

Is Your Home Solar-Ready?

A quick survey confirms it — but here is what makes a great rooftop.

01

Enough Space

About 100 sq.ft. of shadow-free roof per kW — a typical 3 kW home system needs ~300 sq.ft.

02

Sunlight Access

South-facing and shadow-free between 9 AM-4 PM gives the best year-round generation.

03

Sound Structure

A standard RCC terrace easily carries the lightweight ~15 kg/sq.m. mounted system.

04

A Bill Worth It

Homes with monthly bills from ₹2,500 upward see the strongest, fastest returns.
